2D game inspired by the manga “Promised Neverland”

In 2021 I followed the minor Applied Game Design which was also my first encounter with game design and development. For the first project we had to build a 2D platform game in five weeks. Pressure was on, but it was hella fun. This minor showed me a different approach to interaction design which I found really interesting.

I was very intrigued by the storytelling of the manga “Promised Neverland” that made the starting point for my game; The Orphans. The orphans tells the story about Conny who’s one of the ten orphans that escaped from the Paddy Field orphanage after they found out they life in a world where kids are raised to be livestock for demons. The game starts when Conny is wandering through the creepy forest with the goal to find ingredients in order to create this recipe that needs to end the children livestock for demons. Along the way Conny has to face poisoned vegetation obstacles and demons to collect the ingredients.

This was a individual project, partially self made assets and partially through downloadable asset platforms provided by Unity and Itch. Everything was build in Unity and the audio was originated from different sources.

Research

The main theme of my game consists of combat and collecting items. Therefore I did my research by playing and analyzing games that fits my theme to determine what kind of mechanics I wanted to implement in my game. Based on those findings I created moodboards regarding to the environment, level design and characters to visualize the setting of the game.

Design proces

Based on the moodboards and prior research, the setting was in a dark forest with good amount of platforming which translates to the level designs. Characters based on the story: Conny the orphan an innocent yet powerful girl who urges to survive in the woods and the demons hungry creatures looking for human flesh. Went for manga type of artstyle that's visually not too detailed. Here showcasing the proces of character and level design sketches

The final product

After five weeks of many iterations and testing, I built a game that at first is quite slow paced but it can get challenging later on. The goal is to survive the obstacles and enemies in order to collect all the ingredients.
